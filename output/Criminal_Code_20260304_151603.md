## Criminal Code (RSC 1985, c C-46)

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Criminal Code is Canada's primary federal statute governing criminal law and procedure. It defines criminal offences across a comprehensive range of categories including offences against persons, property, public order, and the state, while also establishing the procedural framework for prosecution, trial, sentencing, and appeals. This legislation serves as the foundation of Canada's criminal justice system and applies throughout all Canadian provinces and territories.

## 4. KEY OBLIGATIONS AND PROHIBITIONS

### A. Structural Overview of Offences (Based on Table of Contents)

The Code establishes prohibitions in the following major categories:

#### Offences Against the State (Part II)
- Treason and offences against the Queen's authority
- Sedition
- Unlawful assemblies and riots
- Piracy
- Offences against air or maritime safety

#### Terrorism (Part II.1)
- Financing of terrorism
- Participating in, facilitating, instructing, or harbouring terrorist activity
- Hoax regarding terrorist activity

#### Firearms and Weapons (Part III)
- Use offences
- Possession offences
- Trafficking offences
- Export and import offences
- Offences relating to cartridge magazines

#### Administration of Justice (Part IV)
- Corruption and disobedience
- Misleading justice
- Escapes and rescues

#### Sexual Offences and Public Morals (Part V)
- Sexual offences
- Offences tending to corrupt morals
- Disorderly conduct
- Nuisances

#### Privacy (Part VI)
- Unauthorized interception of communications

#### Gambling and Sex Work (Part VII)
- Disorderly houses, gaming, and betting
- Offences related to offering, providing, or obtaining sexual services

#### Offences Against the Person (Part VIII)
- Criminal negligence
- Homicide (murder, manslaughter, infanticide)

- Bodily harm offences

#### Conveyance Offences (Part VIII.1)
- Impaired operation offences
- Related investigative matters

#### Property Offences (Part IX)
- Theft
- Robbery and extortion
- Breaking and entering
- Possession of stolen property
- False pretences
- Forgery

#### Fraudulent Transactions (Part X)
- Fraud
- Identity theft and fraud
- Forgery of trademarks
- Secret commissions

#### Property Damage (Part XI)
- Mischief
- Arson
- Cruelty to animals

#### Currency Offences (Part XII)
- Making, possessing, uttering counterfeit currency

#### Proceeds of Crime (Part XII.2)
- Money laundering and related offences

1. **"bodily harm"** - The phrase "more than merely transient or trifling" requires case law interpretation for application to specific circumstances.

2. **"firearm"** - The inclusion of "anything that can be adapted for use as a firearm" creates potential ambiguity requiring judicial interpretation.

3. **"mental disorder"** - Defined simply as "disease of the mind," which is a legal rather than medical standard requiring case law analysis.

4. **"common-law partner"** - The one-year cohabitation requirement may raise evidentiary issues in some cases.
