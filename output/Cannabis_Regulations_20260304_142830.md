# Canadian Legal Research Package: Cannabis Regulations (SOR/2018-144)

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Cannabis Regulations establish the comprehensive federal licensing and regulatory framework for the legal cannabis industry in Canada under the Cannabis Act. These regulations govern who can cultivate, process, sell, and research cannabis, setting detailed requirements for licensing, security, product quality, packaging, labelling, and medical access. The regulations create multiple classes of licences (cultivation, processing, analytical testing, sale, research, and cannabis drug licences) with specific subclasses, and establish strict controls on how cannabis products must be manufactured, tested, and distributed throughout the legal supply chain.

## 4. KEY OBLIGATIONS AND PROHIBITIONS

### A. Pre-Licensing Obligations (Section 7)

**Before applying for a licence, applicants MUST:**
- Provide written notice to the local government where the site is located
- Provide written notice to the local fire authority
- Provide written notice to the local police force or RCMP detachment
- Address notices to a senior official of each authority
- Include: applicant's name, expected application date, licence class/subclass, proposed activity, and site address

### B. Possession Requirements (Section 10)

**Licence holders MUST:**
- Only possess cannabis obtained through authorized channels:
  - Former Access to Cannabis for Medical Purposes Regulations
  - Former or current Industrial Hemp Regulations
  - These Cannabis Regulations
  - Provincial retailers authorized under s. 69(1) of the Act

**Exception:** Cultivation licence holders may possess pre-existing plants/seeds if declared in their application

### C. Cultivation Licence Obligations

**Micro-cultivation licence holders (Section 13) MUST:**
- Clearly delineate a surface area not exceeding 800 m²
- Contain ALL cannabis plants (including all parts) within that area
- Include ALL surfaces in area calculations (including stacked surfaces)

**Nursery licence holders cultivating for seeds (Section 16) MUST:**
- Delineate total budding/flowering plant area not exceeding 200 m²
- Not possess more than 20 kg of flowering heads (excluding seeds)
- Destroy flowering heads, leaves, and branches within 30 days of harvest

**All cultivation licence holders (Sections 12, 15) MUST:**
- Retain one individual as "master grower"
- Ensure master grower is responsible for cultivation, propagation, and harvesting
- Ensure master grower has sufficient knowledge of the Act and Regulations

### D. Processing Licence Obligations

**All processing licence holders (Section 19) MUST:**
- Retain a quality assurance person with appropriate training, experience, and technical knowledge
- If conducting edible cannabis activities, retain additional qualified personnel if the primary QA person lacks relevant expertise

**Quality assurance person responsibilities:**
- Assure cannabis quality before sale
- Investigate all quality complaints
- Immediately take measures to mitigate risks when necessary
- Report suspected contamination or quality issues

### E. Sale Restrictions

**Authorized sales channels vary by licence type:**

| Licence Type | May Sell To |
|-------------|-------------|
| Micro/Standard Cultivation | Other cultivators, processors, analytical testing, research, cannabis drug licence holders, Minister, exemption holders, federal/provincial labs |
| Nursery | All above plus other nurseries |
| Micro/Standard Processing | Processors, analytical testing, research, cannabis drug licence holders, Minister, exemption holders, federal/provincial labs, retail licence holders, provincial retailers |

### F. Prohibitions

**Section 5.3 - Voluntary Recall Prohibition:**
- Provincial retailers MUST NOT sell cannabis products they know are subject to voluntary recall for:
  - Quality reasons
  - Non-compliance with Part 5 or 6 requirements

**Section 5.4 - Reference Standard Prohibition:**
- Reference standards MUST NOT be:
  - Used as ingredients
  - Included in cannabis products
  - Packaged/labelled for retail sale

**Section 5.1 - Caffeine Restrictions:**
- Edible cannabis products may contain caffeine ONLY if:
  - Caffeine is from ingredients that naturally contain it
  - Total caffeine per immediate container does not exceed 30 mg

**Section 5.2 - Ethyl Alcohol Restrictions:**
- Cannabis extracts for inhalation must meet s. 101.3(5.1) requirements
- Cannabis extracts for ingestion must meet s. 101.3(6) requirements
- Edible cannabis may contain ethyl alcohol only if concentration ≤ 0.5% w/w
- Cannabis topicals may contain only denatured ethyl alcohol

**Micro-processing Limitation (Section 17(2)(b)(i)):**
- Micro-processing licence holders CANNOT obtain cannabis by synthesis
