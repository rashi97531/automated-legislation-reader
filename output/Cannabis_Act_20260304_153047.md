# CANADIAN LEGISLATIVE RESEARCH PACKAGE

## Cannabis Act (SC 2018, c 16) and Related Documents

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Cannabis Act establishes a comprehensive legal framework for the legalization and regulation of cannabis in Canada for recreational and medical purposes. The Act creates a licensing system for commercial production, distribution, and sale of cannabis while prohibiting possession by those under 18, limiting personal possession quantities, restricting promotion and marketing, and establishing criminal penalties for violations such as illegal sales, trafficking, and providing cannabis to minors.

## 4. KEY OBLIGATIONS AND PROHIBITIONS

### A. PROHIBITIONS ON INDIVIDUALS (18+ years)

**Possession (s. 8):**
- Cannot possess more than 30g equivalent of dried cannabis in public
- Cannot possess any cannabis known to be illicit cannabis
- Cannot possess budding/flowering cannabis plants in public
- Cannot possess more than 4 non-budding/flowering cannabis plants

**Distribution (s. 9):**
- Cannot distribute more than 30g equivalent
- Cannot distribute to anyone under 18
- Cannot distribute to organizations
- Cannot distribute illicit cannabis
- Cannot distribute budding/flowering plants
- Cannot distribute more than 4 non-budding/flowering plants

**Production/Cultivation (s. 12):**
- Cannot cultivate plants from illicit seeds/plant material
- Cannot cultivate more than 4 plants per dwelling-house (regardless of number of adult occupants)
- Cannot cultivate anywhere except dwelling-house without authorization
- Cannot use organic solvents (butane, propane, etc.) for extraction without authorization

### B. PROHIBITIONS ON YOUNG PERSONS (under 18)

- Cannot possess more than 5g equivalent of dried cannabis (s. 8(1)(c))
- Cannot distribute more than 5g equivalent (s. 9(1)(b))
- Cannot cultivate any cannabis plants (s. 12(7))

### C. ABSOLUTE PROHIBITIONS (all persons)

**Sales (s. 10):**
- Selling cannabis without authorization is prohibited to anyone (adult, minor, or organization)

**Import/Export (s. 11):**
- Importation and exportation prohibited without authorization

**Using Young Persons (s. 14):**
- Using young persons in commission of cannabis offences is prohibited

### D. PROMOTION AND MARKETING PROHIBITIONS (ss. 17-24)

**Prohibited promotion includes:**
- Promotion appealing to young persons
- Testimonials or endorsements
- Depiction of persons, characters, or animals
- Lifestyle associations (glamour, excitement, risk, daring)
- False or misleading promotion
- Sponsorship of persons, events, or facilities
- Certain terms specified in regulations

**Exceptions exist for:**
- Age-gated communications
- Places where young persons prohibited by law
- Business-to-business promotion

### E. PACKAGING AND LABELLING REQUIREMENTS (ss. 25-28)

- Must comply with regulations
- Cannot use packaging appealing to young persons
- Cannot use testimonials, character depictions, lifestyle associations
- Cannot use false or misleading information

### F. DISPLAY AND SALES RESTRICTIONS (ss. 29-37)

- Cannot display cannabis visible to young persons
- Cannot sell cannabis appealing to young persons
- Cannot sell cannabis accessories to young persons
- Cannot sell cannabis not listed in Schedule 4
- Cannot sell recalled cannabis
- No self-service displays
- No dispensing devices (vending machines)

### G. LICENSING OBLIGATIONS (Cannabis Regulations)

Licensed producers must:
- Meet physical security requirements (Part 4)
- Follow Good Production Practices (Part 5)
- Meet product standards (Part 6)
- Comply with packaging/labelling requirements (Part 7)
- Maintain records (Part 11)
- Submit reports (Part 12)
- Obtain security clearances for key personnel (Part 3)

## 5. PENALTIES AND ENFORCEMENT

### A. CRIMINAL PENALTIES

| Offence | Maximum Penalty (Indictable) | Maximum Penalty (Summary) |
|---------|------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Possession violations** (s. 8) | 5 years less a day (individuals); discretionary fine (organizations) | $5,000 and/or 6 months (individuals); $100,000 (organizations) |
| **Distribution violations** (s. 9) | 14 years | $5,000 and/or 6 months (general); $15,000 and/or 18 months (to minors) |
| **Illegal sales** (s. 10) | 14 years | $5,000 and/or 6 months (general); $15,000 and/or 18 months (to minors) |
| **Import/Export violations** (s. 11) | 14 years | $5,000 and/or 6 months; $300,000 (organizations) |
| **Illegal production** (s. 12) | 14 years | $5,000 and/or 6 months; $100,000 (organizations) |
| **Possession for illicit production/distribution** (s. 13) | 7 years | $5,000 and/or 6 months; $100,000 (organizations) |
| **Using young persons** (s. 14) | 14 years | $15,000 and/or 18 months; $100,000 (organizations) |

### B. AGGRAVATING SENTENCING FACTORS (s. 15)

Courts must consider:
- Use of weapons or violence
- Sales near schools or places frequented by young persons
- Prior cannabis or controlled substance convictions

### C. YOUNG PERSON OFFENDERS

- Subject to Youth Criminal Justice Act
- Extrajudicial measures available (s. 5.1)

### D. TICKETABLE OFFENCES (Part 2, s. 51)

- Certain minor offences may be dealt with by ticket (detailed provisions not included in excerpt)

### E. ADMINISTRATIVE MONETARY PENALTIES (Part 10)

- Minister may impose AMPs for regulatory violations
- Compliance agreements available
- Review process established

### F. FORFEITURE AND SEIZURE (Parts 8-9)

- Search warrants available
- Restraint and management orders for offence-related property
- Forfeiture of cannabis and chemical property

### G. LICENSING CONSEQUENCES

- Licence refusal, suspension, or revocation for non-compliance
- Security clearance denial or revocation

### H. MEDICAL EMERGENCY EXEMPTION (s. 8.1)

**Immunity from prosecution when:**
- Person seeks emergency assistance for overdose
- Evidence of possession obtained as result of seeking help
- Applies to person seeking help, person suffering emergency, and bystanders
- Also protects against pre-trial release/probation condition violations
